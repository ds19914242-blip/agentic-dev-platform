# Specification Contract Model

## Definition

A Specification is a structured description of intended work.

Specifications may vary in format, detail and purpose.

The system does not enforce a fixed specification structure.

The system enforces a minimum Specification Contract.

## Purpose

The Specification Contract ensures that planning and implementation are based on sufficient information.

## Required Questions

Every Specification must answer:

### Problem

What problem is being solved?

### Goal

What outcome is expected?

### Users

Who benefits from the result?

### Scope

What is included?

### Out of Scope

What is intentionally excluded?

### Success Criteria

How will success be measured?

### Constraints

What limitations or requirements exist?

### Risks

What known risks exist?

## Optional Sections

Examples:

- Business Model
- Monetization
- Competitive Analysis
- User Journeys
- Technical Notes
- Security Requirements
- Compliance Requirements

## Validation

The Orchestrator must validate that required questions are answered.

The format is flexible.

The contract is mandatory.

## Rules

Specifications may be written by humans or agents.

Specifications may evolve during execution.

Changes must be versioned and recorded.

Implementation must not begin until the Specification Contract is satisfied.
